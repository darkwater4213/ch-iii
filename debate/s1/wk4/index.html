On Various Governmental Systems Present in the Thirteen Colonies and their Multiple Failing Points

Plymouth, Massachusetts, 1620. Governor John Carver of the Plymouth colony in New England has just been nominated as such. In the words of William Bradford, the next governor of Plymouth, his first actions were to "[take] away property, and [bring] community into a common wealth... [it] would make [the colonists] happy and flourishing". This resulted in "young men that were most fit and able for labor and service did repine that they should spend their time and strength to work for other men's wives and children without any recompense... was thought injustice."

This system was entirely inadequate for the citizens of Plymouth.
