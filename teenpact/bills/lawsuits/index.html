Vexatious Lawsuit Prevention Act

State of Washington 68th Legislature 2023 Regular Session

By House Judicial (originally sponsored by Representatives Anthony, Duarte, and Myhre)

AN ACT Relating to reforming the civil suit system; and adding new sections to RCW ---.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON:

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 1. For the purposes of this act, “frivolous suit” refers to any lawsuit that has no legal basis; "vexatious lawsuit" refers to any suit leveled for the purpose of harassment and without probable cause.

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 2. All lawyers' fees, court fees, etc. associated with any resolved suit between two individual citizens of the United States of America shall be paid by the loser of the lawsuit.

If the plaintiff is a noncitizen, such as a corporation, and the defendant is an individual citizen, then the defendant is exempt from needing to pay the plaintiff's lawyers' fees in the event of the plaintiff succeeding in the lawsuit. However, the corporation must still pay the citizen's lawyers' fees in the event that the corporation loses the lawsuit.

A suit between two corporations is to be treated the same as a suit between two individual citizens, that is, the loser must pay all lawyers' fees.

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 3. Any defendant demonstrably unable to fulfill such a monetary obligation is allowed to immediately ask during the proceedings to settle out of court. In all other cases, requests to settle out of court must be made in the proper manner.

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 4. All laws or parts of laws in conflict with this act are hereby repealed.

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 5. This act shall become effective 30 days after approval by the Governor or its otherwise becoming a law.

    NEW SECTION.
Sec. 6. Upon approval by the Governor, this bill will be reviewed by the Appropriation Committee for funding.
